Scope — which pages get the editorial + glass treatment?

We could redesign everything at once, or stage it. Pick the scope that matches how hard you want to push. The visual effort per page is similar; what changes is reach and risk.

A

Homepage only

The single highest-traffic surface gets the new treatment. Everything else (workers, machines, certificates, dashboard, upload/approve/audit) stays on the current calmer version. Lowest risk, fastest to ship, gives you a before/after to evaluate before committing to the rest.

~1 hero + ~9 landing components to rework.

B

All public-facing pages

Homepage, Live Network, Workers (list + profile), Machines (list + profile), Leaderboard, Certificates, Industries, and the 404. Internal/workflow pages (Upload, Approve, Audit, Dashboard) stay calmer since they need maximum focus, not visual flair. Best balance of reach and restraint.

~12 page surfaces. Dashboard stays as-is for now.

C

Every page, end to end

Full redesign across all 11 routes including workflow pages and dashboard. The platform feels coherent everywhere — no jarring switch when a worker goes from the marketing surface to upload evidence. Highest effort, but the most "this is the new MaintChain" outcome.

~12 page surfaces + form-heavy workflow pages + dashboard.

D

Staged: marketing first, internals after

Ship A first (homepage). After we both agree it works, do B in a follow-up release. Dashboard, Upload, Approve, and Audit are deferred to a third pass or kept on the current design. Recommended for production stability.

Two releases. Less risk, slower to a fully unified look.

My recommendation: B. The workflow pages (Upload, Approve, Audit) are form-heavy and benefit from the calmer current design — pushing glass + numbered editorial style onto them would compete with the form content. Dashboard is a judgment call (it has reputation stats that would look great in glass), so we can revisit it.
